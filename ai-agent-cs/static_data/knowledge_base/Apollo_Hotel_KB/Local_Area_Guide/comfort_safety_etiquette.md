# Guest Comfort, Safety & Etiquette

## 💧 Hygiene & Health
- **Tap Water:** Do **not** drink the tap water in Hanoi. Use the complimentary bottled water provided in your room for drinking and brushing teeth.
- **Street Food:** Hanoi is famous for its street food! Look for stalls with high turnover—this usually indicates the food is fresh.

## 🛡️ Safety in the City
- **Pickpockets:** Be mindful of your belongings in crowded areas like the **Old Quarter** or **Night Markets**. Keep your bag in front of you.
- **Street Crossing:** Traffic in Hanoi can be intimidating! Walk at a slow, steady pace across the street. Drivers will predict your path and go around you. Do **not** stop suddenly or run.

## 🏛️ Local Etiquette
- **Temple Dress Code:** When visiting pagodas and temples (like the Temple of Literature), please ensure your **shoulders and knees are covered**.
- **Tipping:** Tipping is not mandatory but highly appreciated for exceptional service.
  - *Small services:* 10,000 – 20,000 VND.
  - *Tour guides/Drivers:* 50,000 – 100,000 VND.
- **Photos:** Always ask for permission before taking photographs of local residents.

## 🌡️ Weather Advice
- **Humidity:** Hanoi can be very humid. We recommend keeping your room balcony doors closed to ensure the AC system keeps your room cool and dry. ❄️
